# Postmortem: KCA 891H Front-Left Tyre Failure (May 2026)

## Incident Summary
- **Date**: 2026-05-14, 11:20 EAT
- **Vehicle**: KCA 891H (Mombasa → Malindi route)
- **Driver**: Omar Hassan
- **Location**: Nyali Bridge, approaching Mombasa
- **Severity**: HIGH — vehicle disabled, no injuries

## What Happened
Driver reported a loud impact followed by immediate steering pull to the left. He brought the vehicle to a controlled stop on the shoulder. Front-left tyre showed sidewall split and cord exposure. Diagnosis: pothole impact at speed (estimated 55 km/h based on telematics).

## Root Cause Analysis
**Direct cause**: undetected pothole approximately 15cm deep on the approach to Nyali Bridge. The road surface in this section has degraded significantly during the 2026 rainy season.

**Contributing factors**:
- Tyre had 4mm tread remaining — within spec but near the rotation threshold
- Tyre pressure measured 0.3 bar below recommended at the post-incident check (insufficient cushioning to absorb the impact)
- No prior route advisory existed for this section of road

## Resolution
- Recovery dispatched within 35 minutes (target: 30)
- Tyre replaced on-site by Tyre Centre Mombasa (service record SVC-003)
- Vehicle returned to service the same day

## Lessons & Actions
1. **Route advisory**: Nyali Bridge approach added to the "rough surface" route advisory list. Drivers cautioned to reduce speed to 40 km/h until road repairs complete.
2. **Tyre rotation threshold**: Lowered from 3mm to 4mm for vehicles assigned to the Mombasa route, where road quality is more variable.
3. **Pre-trip pressure checks**: Mandatory for all Mombasa-bound vehicles, recorded in the dispatch log.
4. **Sister-tyre inspection**: Front-right and rear-left tyres on KCA 891H also showed minor sidewall scuffing from the impact. Scheduled for inspection at the next service.
