# Engine Overheating — Response Procedure

## Immediate Action (Driver)
If the temperature gauge enters the red zone or you see steam from the bonnet:
1. Switch off air conditioning. Turn cabin heater to maximum — this draws heat away from the engine.
2. Pull over safely. Do NOT continue driving — sustained overheating warps cylinder heads within minutes and can crack the engine block.
3. Switch off engine. Wait minimum 30 minutes before lifting the bonnet.
4. NEVER remove the radiator cap on a hot engine. Pressurised coolant escapes violently and causes severe burns.
5. Call dispatch and the recovery line.

## Diagnostic Checklist (Mechanic, after cooldown)
- **Coolant level** — top up if low, then check for visible leaks at hoses, radiator, water pump, and engine block.
- **Radiator fins** — look for blockage (insects, debris, mud). Clean with low-pressure water if obstructed.
- **Radiator fan** — should spin reliably at 90-95°C. Test by jumping the fan relay.
- **Thermostat** — stuck closed traps coolant in the engine. Replace if temperature climbs rapidly with no fan response.
- **Coolant condition** — rust suggests internal corrosion; oil in coolant indicates head gasket failure.
- **Water pump** — check bearing play (any wobble = replace) and pulley alignment.
- **Head gasket** — white exhaust smoke, bubbles in coolant, milky engine oil are all symptoms.

## Common Root Causes (by frequency)
1. Failed thermostat — most common single-point cause
2. Low coolant from a slow external leak
3. Radiator fan electrical fault
4. Blocked radiator core (older vehicles, dusty routes)
5. Water pump failure
6. Head gasket failure — least common but most expensive

## After Repair
Always pressure-test the cooling system before returning the vehicle to service. A leak that develops after the initial repair is often missed without this step.
