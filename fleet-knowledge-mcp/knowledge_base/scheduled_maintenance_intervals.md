# Scheduled Maintenance Intervals

## Time-Based Services
- **30-day inspection** — visual check, fluids top-up. Standard fleet practice; performed at the depot.
- **90-day service** — oil change, filter replacement, brake inspection, tyre rotation.
- **180-day service** — 90-day service plus transmission fluid check, suspension inspection.
- **365-day service** (annual) — full inspection, coolant flush, brake fluid replacement, timing belt inspection.

## Mileage-Based Services (whichever comes first)
- **15,000 km** — oil and filter change.
- **30,000 km** — air filter, cabin filter, brake pad measurement.
- **60,000 km** — timing belt inspection, spark plugs, fuel filter.
- **120,000 km** — timing belt replacement, transmission service, full driveline inspection.

## Condition-Based Triggers
Regardless of schedule, the following warrant immediate service:
- Brake pad warning indicator activated
- Engine warning light, any code
- Tyre wear approaching 2.5mm at any measurement point
- Unusual noises, vibrations, or smells reported by the driver
- Fuel economy degradation of more than 15% from the rolling baseline
- Any single fault that caused a road incident

## Service Lead Time
Routine services should be scheduled at least 7 days in advance to allow garage capacity planning. High-priority services (incident-related or condition-based) must be scheduled within 24-48 hours and bypass the routine queue.

## Tracking
The fleet maintenance system maintains the next-due service date for each vehicle. Vehicles within 7 days of their due date appear on the weekly planning report; vehicles overdue appear on the daily exception report and trigger an alert to the operations manager.
