# Tyre Replacement Procedure

## When to Replace
Replace any tyre that:
- Has tread depth below 1.6mm at any measurement point
- Shows sidewall cuts, bulges, or cord exposure
- Has been subjected to a sharp impact (kerbs, deep potholes)
- Shows uneven wear that cannot be corrected by rotation
- Has aged beyond 6 years from manufacture date, regardless of tread depth

## Standard Procedure
1. Park on level ground. Apply parking brake. Chock the opposite wheel.
2. Loosen wheel nuts before lifting. Lift vehicle on hydraulic jack and place on axle stands.
3. Remove wheel. Inspect brake components, wheel arch, and adjacent suspension for damage from the failure event.
4. Mount new tyre. Inflate to manufacturer specification before fitting to vehicle.
5. Fit wheel and tighten nuts hand-tight. Lower vehicle until weight rests on wheel.
6. Torque nuts in a star (criss-cross) pattern to the manufacturer specification — typically 110-140 Nm for light commercial vehicles, 200-250 Nm for heavy goods.
7. Lower vehicle fully. Re-torque nuts after 50km of driving — wheel nuts can settle and loosen during the first journey.

## After Pothole or Impact Damage
Always inspect:
- Steering alignment (drift on straight road indicates damage)
- Suspension control arms and bushings for visible deformation
- Adjacent wheels for impact stress (cracks or warping)
- The rim itself — even invisible cracks can cause slow leaks

If the impact was severe, all four wheels and the steering geometry should be checked, not just the failed tyre.
