# Diagnosing a Faulty Fuel Injector

## Symptoms
A faulty fuel injector typically manifests as:
- Engine stuttering or hesitation, especially at low RPM
- Reduced fuel economy (10-25% drop)
- Rough idling
- Unusual exhaust smell (fuel-rich or sulphurous)
- Check engine light with codes in the P0200-P0299 range

## Diagnostic Procedure
1. Connect OBD-II scanner. Pull all stored codes.
2. Listen to each injector with a mechanic's stethoscope while the engine idles. A working injector produces a steady clicking sound.
3. Measure injector resistance with a multimeter. Typical reading is 11-17 ohms. Out-of-range values indicate a coil fault.
4. Use a noid light to verify the ECU is sending the correct pulse to each injector.
5. If symptoms persist, perform a balance test: each cylinder should produce equal power. A weak cylinder usually points to the matching injector.

## Common Causes
- Contaminated fuel (water, debris) blocking the injector tip
- Carbon buildup distorting the injector spray pattern
- Failed solenoid coil within the injector body
- Wiring harness damage causing intermittent connection

## Resolution
Cleaning is often sufficient for partial blockages — ultrasonic cleaning at a workshop is more effective than fuel additives. Full injector replacement is required for coil failures or persistent blockages after cleaning.

If multiple injectors fail together, suspect contaminated fuel from the most recent fill — investigate the fuel source.
